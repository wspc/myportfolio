Infographic Design Project

Which is the healthiest cereal for athletes?

Year /Time Take

2013/ 1 Week

Tools

Adobe Illustrator

Background

The concept of this infograph is to present the healthiest cereal primarily by comparing the differences in the amount of sugar in one serving (1 cup) of cereal. The sugar levels per serving is shown in the different lengths of the spoon handle, with the healthiest cereal the longest handle.

As the amount of sugar increase, the spoon handle gets smaller. By providing an annoyingly short eating utensil, the graph shows the unhealthy cereals should be eaten less. Cereals with 0 grams of sugar per serving are sorted by their secondary nutritional attributes, protein and vitamins.

In this graph, I applied the LATCH principles by using categories to differentiate between grams of sugar, protein, the percentage of vitamins. I also used ranking in ascending order to establish a hierarchy from least healthy to the healthiest cereals. The Gestalt Principle of similarity is used in the shapes of the spoons to separate the cereals into clusters with similar characteristics.
